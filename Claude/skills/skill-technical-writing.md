# Skill: Technical Writing

Covers papers, abstracts, methods sections, proposal text, and research summaries.

## Format

- **Papers / methods sections**: LaTeX-ready prose. Dense, precise, logically sequenced. Each paragraph should advance one clear idea. Notation should be consistent and defined on first use.
- **Abstracts**: 150–250 words unless otherwise specified. Lead with the problem, state the approach, summarize the key result or contribution, close with significance or implication. No filler sentences.
- **Research summaries**: Markdown. Structured by problem → method → result → implication. Concise enough to skim, precise enough to cite.
- **Proposal text**: Clean prose, not bullet points. See `skill-proposal-framing.md` for framing logic.

## Voice Rules

- Direct, precise, technically dense but logically legible.
- No hype language. No "novel," "revolutionary," "game-changing," or inflated claims of novelty.
- Frame contributions in terms of what the method actually does, what assumptions it makes, and why it matters scientifically.
- Use Jarett's natural vocabulary: "physically meaningful," "mechanistically grounded," "sparse-data setting," "structure-property relationships."
- Keep claims proportional to evidence. If something is preliminary, say so.

## Process

1. Ask what the piece is for (journal, proposal, internal report) and who the audience is.
2. If editing existing text: make surgical changes. Do not rewrite what doesn't need rewriting.
3. If drafting from scratch: produce a strong, complete first pass — not a tentative skeleton.
4. Flag any sentence where the factual basis is uncertain or where a citation would be needed.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Every paragraph advances one clear idea
- [ ] No filler sentences or padding
- [ ] Claims are proportional to evidence
- [ ] Technical terms are used precisely and consistently
- [ ] Notation is defined on first use
- [ ] No hype language or overclaiming
- [ ] Physically meaningful framing — not just statistical performance
- [ ] Uncertainty and limitations are stated honestly
- [ ] Any fabricated or unverifiable claim is flagged explicitly
